Frequently Asked Questions

What triggers a tax audit?

+

Tax audits can be triggered by various factors including high income, business losses, unusual deductions, foreign income, property transactions, or random selection. The Income Tax Department uses various criteria to select returns for audit.

How long does an audit take?

+

The duration of an audit varies depending on its complexity. Simple audits may take 2-3 months, while complex cases can extend to 6-12 months. Our team works to expedite the process while ensuring thorough compliance.

What documents do I need for an audit?

+

Required documents include income proofs, investment certificates, bank statements, property documents, business records (if applicable), and any other documents related to your income and deductions. We provide a comprehensive checklist.

Can I represent myself in an audit?

+

While you can represent yourself, it's not recommended. Professional representation ensures proper handling of complex tax matters, reduces stress, and often leads to better outcomes. Our experts understand audit procedures and can effectively advocate on your behalf.

What happens if I disagree with audit findings?

+

If you disagree with audit findings, you have the right to appeal. Our team can help you file appeals, represent you in hearings, and work towards a favorable resolution. We provide complete support throughout the appeal process.
